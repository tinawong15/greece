GOLDEN DAWN
  • History
  • Electorate
  • Conflict
  • Impact



December 1980

Michaloliakos begins the publication Chrysí Avgí, which praises Nazism for its connection to classical Greek philosophical ideals.

April 1984

Michaloliakos becomes leader of the National Political Union’s youth section, a party founded by Georgios Papadopoulos, the head of the military junta that previously ruled Greece.

January 1985

Michaloliakos leaves the National Political Union and starts the Golden Dawn party, described as a “Popular National Movement.”

1993

Golden Dawn is officially recognized as a political party.



1994

Golden Dawn begins running in elections, but attracts little support.

2010

Michaloliakos is elected to the Athens City Council.


May 2012

The party runs a campaign during the Greek national elections based on concerns about unemployment, austerity, the economy, and immigration.

September 2013

A party supporter murders Pavlos Fyssas. Many Golden Dawn members are arrested.

October 2013

Greek Parliament votes to cut state funding for the Golden Dawn party, 235 to 0.

April 2015

Trial begins and is still ongoing.
